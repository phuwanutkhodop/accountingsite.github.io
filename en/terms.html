The rules that apply when you read what we publish here, and what we mean — and don't mean — when we publish it.

Last updated: 26 April 2026

About this notice. This page sets out the terms that apply when you use the website of Your Firm Name (the "firm," "we," "us," or "our"). By using the site, you agree to these terms. If you do not agree with them, please do not use the site.

1. Who we are

Your Firm Name is a Thailand-registered advisory firm based in Bangkok. We provide tax, accounting, and onboarding services to foreign-owned businesses operating in Thailand and to foreign companies preparing to enter the Thai market.

2. About this website

This website exists to introduce the firm and to share articles, guides, and commentary on the topics we work on. Most of the content here is written for an audience of foreign founders, executives, and finance leaders who are considering or already operating in Thailand.

We try to keep the site accurate, useful, and reasonably up to date. We don't promise that every article will reflect the latest position on every point of Thai law, regulation, or practice — laws change, regulations are reinterpreted, and our articles reflect the position at the time we wrote them. The publication date is shown on every article so you can judge for yourself.

3. The articles are not advice

Nothing on this website is legal, tax, accounting, or professional advice. The articles, guides, and commentary we publish here are general explanations of how things work in Thailand for foreign-owned businesses. They are not specific advice on your situation, your company, or your transaction.

Specific advice depends on the specific facts. The same question — about a tax filing, a work permit, a shareholding structure — can have a different answer depending on the company's history, the people involved, the timing, and a dozen other things that don't fit into a public article.

If you are facing a real decision and the answer matters, please don't rely on what we publish here. Please write to us, or speak with another qualified advisor, so the answer you act on is shaped to your situation rather than to a general audience.

Reading this website does not create a client relationship between you and the firm. A client relationship begins only when we have agreed an engagement in writing.

4. When AI assistants quote our work

Our articles are written for the public web, and they are increasingly read — and paraphrased — by AI search engines and assistants. We are glad when our work is useful in those contexts.

A paraphrase by a third party is not advice from the firm. When an AI assistant summarises one of our articles, the summary belongs to the assistant, not to us. If you read a summary of our work elsewhere and the question matters, please come back to the original article, check the date, and — if the answer affects a real decision — write to us.
