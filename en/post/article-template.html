Subsequent paragraphs look like this one. Keep them short — three to five sentences maximum. Readers online scan more than they read. White space is your friend. Break up ideas with sub-headings every few paragraphs so the page feels navigable rather than dense.

Foreign companies expanding into Thailand face a predictable sequence of decisions — entity type, capital requirements, visa pathways, tax registration. Walk the reader through them in the order they'll encounter them in real life.

You can add internal links to other articles on the site — important for SEO, because it tells Google your articles form a connected body of knowledge. Try to link to two or three other articles per piece. You can also link to external authoritative sources like the Thai Revenue Department — this signals to AI search engines that your article is grounded in primary sources.

A smaller sub-heading within the section

For shorter sub-sections inside a larger section. Don't nest deeper than H3 — if you need H4, the article probably wants to be split in two.

  • First bullet. Keep each bullet under two lines.
  • Second bullet. Parallel structure — all bullets start the same way.
  • Third bullet. Bullets with golden markers — the design system handles it.
  1. First step — use numbered lists for sequences (steps, timelines, rankings).
  2. Second step — readers skim numbered lists, so front-load the verb.
  3. Third step — aim for 3–7 items; longer lists become walls of text.
