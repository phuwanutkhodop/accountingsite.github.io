Privacy

How we handle your information.

A plain-English summary of what we collect, why we collect it, and the rights you have over it. Written for the people who actually have to read this.

Last updated: 26 April 2026

About this notice. This page explains how Your Firm Name (the "firm," "we," "us," or "our") handles personal information collected through this website and through our professional engagements. If anything here is unclear, or you would like more detail about how we hold information about you specifically, please contact us using the details at the bottom of this page.

1. Who we are

Your Firm Name is a Thailand-registered advisory firm based in Bangkok. We provide tax, accounting, and onboarding services to foreign-owned businesses operating in Thailand, and to foreign companies preparing to enter the Thai market.

For the purposes of this notice, the firm is the data controller of personal information collected through this website and through our engagements with clients and prospective clients.

2. What information we collect

Information you give us directly

When you contact us by email, telephone, or in person — or when you become a client of the firm — you may share your name, your role, your employer, your contact details, and information about your business situation that is relevant to the question or engagement.

Information we collect automatically

When you visit this website, our hosting and analytics services may record technical information about your visit: your IP address, your browser type, the pages you view, the time you spent on each page, and the page that referred you here. We do not use this information to identify individual visitors.

Information we receive from third parties

In the course of an engagement, we may receive information about you from your employer, your colleagues, your other professional advisors, or from public registers (such as the Thai Department of Business Development).

3. How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • respond to enquiries you send us;
  • deliver the services you have engaged us to provide;
  • communicate with you about ongoing matters;
  • meet our legal, regulatory, and professional obligations (including record-keeping obligations under Thai law and anti-money-laundering rules);
  • improve this website and the way we communicate;
  • send occasional updates about our work, only if you have asked to receive them.

4. Why we are allowed to use your information

Under Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), we process personal information on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • your consent, where you have asked us to do something specific (such as send you an article);
  • the performance of a contract with you, where you have engaged the firm for a specific piece of work;
  • our legitimate interests in running and promoting a professional services firm, where those interests are not outweighed by your rights;
  • compliance with a legal obligation the firm is subject to (for example, tax record-keeping, audit evidence retention, AML/KYC obligations).

If you are a visitor from the United Kingdom or the European Union, the equivalent legal bases under the UK GDPR or EU GDPR apply. We work with foreign-owned businesses regularly, and the substance of these protections is the same.

5. Who we share your information with

We do not sell personal information, and we do not share it for marketing purposes. We may share information with:

  • Service providers who support the firm's operations (such as our IT and email providers, and our secure document storage providers), under written confidentiality obligations.
  • Government authorities and regulators where we are required to do so by law — for example, when filing tax returns, statutory accounts, audit reports, or responding to a lawful order.
  • Other professional advisors involved in the same engagement, but only with your knowledge and only to the extent necessary for the work.
  • A successor firm, if the practice is ever transferred or acquired — in which case continuing obligations of confidentiality apply.

6. Where your information is held

The firm is based in Thailand and most of the information we hold is processed in Thailand. Some service providers we use (for example, email and document-storage providers) may store information on servers located outside Thailand, including in jurisdictions whose data-protection regimes may differ from your home jurisdiction.

Where information is transferred outside Thailand, we take steps to ensure that an appropriate level of protection is maintained — typically by selecting service providers with recognised security and privacy practices and contractual confidentiality obligations.

7. How long we keep your information

We keep personal information only for as long as we need it for the purposes set out in this notice — and, where the law requires, for the minimum period the law specifies.

For client engagement records, we typically retain working files for the period required under Thai accounting and professional rules. For website visitor data and general enquiries, we retain information for no longer than is reasonably necessary.

8. Your rights

Under the PDPA — and, for visitors from the UK and EU, under the equivalent provisions of the UK GDPR and EU GDPR — you have the right to:

  • ask whether we hold personal information about you, and request a copy of it;
  • ask us to correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete;
  • ask us to delete information we no longer need;
  • ask us to restrict how we use your information in certain circumstances;
  • object to our use of your information for purposes based on our legitimate interests;
  • withdraw consent at any time, where consent is the basis for our processing;
  • complain to a data-protection authority, including Thailand's Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will normally respond within thirty days.

9. Cookies and website analytics

This website may use lightweight analytics to understand which articles and pages visitors find useful. We do not use analytics that build advertising profiles about you across other websites. We do not use cookies for marketing purposes.

10. Children's information

Our services are aimed at businesses and professional users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us and we will delete it.

11. Changes to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time — typically when our practices change, when the law changes, or when we update the website. The "last updated" date at the top of this page tells you when the most recent change was made. If a change is significant, we will say so plainly at the top of the page.

12. How to contact us

For any privacy question, request, or complaint, please write to us at the email address below. We treat privacy enquiries as a priority and will respond promptly.

Email: hello@yourfirm.com

You may also contact Thailand's Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) directly if you are not satisfied with our response.
